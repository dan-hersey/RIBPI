Bitcoin 101 for Legislators

A nonpartisan guide to understanding Bitcoin and blockchain technology for Rhode Island policymakers.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is Freedom, Security, and Infrastructure for the 21st Century. Bitcoin operates without corporate, governmental, or partisan control. Every transaction is verifiable. The system runs continuously, permissionless, resistant to inflation or censorship.

Core Principles

Bitcoin represents a fundamental innovation in how value can be stored and transferred without intermediaries. It's both a payment network and a form of money, created in 2009 with a market capitalization exceeding $1.5 trillion.

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that allows people to send and receive value directly to each other without needing a bank or other intermediary. Think of it as digital cash that works over the internet.

Key Concept

Bitcoin is both a payment network (like Visa or SWIFT) and a form of money (like dollars or gold). It was created in 2009 and has grown to a market capitalization exceeding $1.5 trillion.

How is Bitcoin Different from Regular Money?

US Dollar Gold Bitcoin
Supply Unlimited - controlled by Federal Reserve Limited but growing (~2% annually from mining) Fixed at 21 million coins
Issuer Federal government Natural occurrence (mined from earth) Decentralized - created by network
Transfer Speed Hours to days via banking system Days to weeks (must be physically shipped) Minutes to hours via internet
Transparency Limited transparency of transactions No public record of transfers Fully transparent - all transactions public and auditable
Inflation Subject to currency devaluation Limited inflation from new mining No inflation - fixed supply ensures scarcity

Bitcoin's supply is mathematically fixed at 21 million coins—a feature that cannot be changed. This scarcity is why some call it "digital gold" and see it as a hedge against inflation.

Why States Should Care

States are increasingly recognizing Bitcoin's importance for economic development and financial innovation. Here are the key reasons to pay attention:

Attract Innovation & Startups

Bitcoin-friendly policies draw tech companies, blockchain developers, and investment capital to the state, creating high-paying jobs in a growing sector.

Establish Tax Clarity

Clear regulatory frameworks for Bitcoin transactions reduce uncertainty for businesses and residents, encouraging legitimate commerce and investment.

Create Resilient Infrastructure

Blockchain technology provides transparent, immutable records for government services, improving efficiency and security of public systems.

Advance Digital-Era Civil Liberties

Protecting citizens' rights to hold and control digital assets aligns with fundamental principles of financial freedom and property rights.

Key Benefits of Bitcoin

Sound Money

Fixed 21 million coin supply. Cannot be devalued through money printing. Bitcoin's scarcity is mathematically guaranteed and cannot be changed.

Financial Sovereignty

Access global commerce without intermediaries. Direct peer-to-peer transfers enable financial inclusion for the unbanked and facilitate low-cost remittances.

Economic Development

Mining, development, and payment services create jobs. States adopting Bitcoin-friendly policies are attracting blockchain companies and high-paying tech positions.

Cybersecurity Infrastructure

Blockchain architecture improves public recordkeeping. Transparent, immutable ledgers enhance security of government records and citizen data.

How Does Bitcoin Work?

Bitcoin operates on a technology called blockchain—a public ledger that records every transaction ever made. This ledger is maintained by thousands of computers around the world, making it virtually impossible to hack or manipulate.

The Basics

  • Wallets: Software that stores your Bitcoin credentials (like a digital bank account you control)
  • Private Keys: Secret codes that prove ownership and authorize transactions (like a PIN, but much more secure)
  • Blockchain: The public record of all transactions—transparent, permanent, and tamper-proof
  • Mining: The process by which transactions are verified and new Bitcoin are created

Why This Matters for Legislators

Bitcoin's transparent blockchain actually makes it easier to trace transactions than cash. Law enforcement agencies have solved major cases specifically because criminals used Bitcoin, which leaves a permanent public record.

A Simple Transaction Example

When Alice sends Bitcoin to Bob:

  1. Alice authorizes the transaction with her private key
  2. The transaction is broadcast to the network
  3. Miners verify the transaction is valid
  4. The transaction is recorded on the blockchain permanently
  5. Bob receives the Bitcoin—typically in under an hour, often in minutes

No bank, no intermediary, no business hours—just a direct transfer between two parties.

Mining: The Heart of Bitcoin Security

Mining is the process that validates transactions and secures the Bitcoin network. Miners invest in specialized hardware and electricity to solve complex mathematical problems. The first to solve the problem gets to add the next block of transactions and receives newly created Bitcoin plus transaction fees.

Why Mining Matters

  • Security: Makes it economically infeasible to attack or manipulate the network
  • Decentralization: Thousands of independent miners worldwide prevent any single entity from controlling the network
  • Economic Incentives: Miners are rewarded for honest participation, aligning self-interest with network security
  • Energy Evolution: Over 50% of mining now uses renewable energy; miners use stranded energy that would otherwise be wasted

Energy Perspective

Bitcoin mining can actually stabilize power grids. In Texas, miners participate in demand response programs, reducing consumption during peak demand. The global banking system uses 2-3x more energy than Bitcoin.

Myth vs Fact

Let's address the concerns legislators most frequently raise about Bitcoin:

❌ Myth: "Bitcoin is mainly used for crime"

Illicit activity represents less than 1% of Bitcoin transactions (Chainalysis data). The US dollar remains the dominant currency for money laundering. Bitcoin's transparent ledger actually helps law enforcement.

✓ Reality

Bitcoin is primarily used for savings, investment, and legitimate commerce. Major corporations like Tesla, MicroStrategy, and Block hold Bitcoin on their balance sheets.

❌ Myth: "Bitcoin wastes energy"

Bitcoin mining uses energy, but over 50% comes from renewable sources. Miners often use stranded energy (flared gas, excess renewables) that would otherwise be wasted.

✓ Reality

Bitcoin mining can actually stabilize power grids. In Texas, miners participate in demand response programs, reducing consumption during peak demand. The global banking system uses 2-3x more energy.

❌ Myth: "Bitcoin is too volatile"

Bitcoin has short-term volatility, but over any 4-year period in its history, it has appreciated significantly. Strategic reserve legislation includes caps and safeguards.

✓ Reality

Volatility decreases as adoption increases. Major institutions (BlackRock, Fidelity) now offer Bitcoin products. Texas started with just $5M—a prudent, measured approach.

What Can Rhode Island Do?

Rhode Island has an opportunity to join leading states in embracing Bitcoin and blockchain technology. Here are the legislative options currently under consideration:

2026 Active Legislation

  • H.7956 + S.2198 — Study Commission: Create a legislative commission to study blockchain applications for government
  • H.7413 — Regulatory Sandbox: Attract blockchain businesses with innovation-friendly regulations and SPDIs
  • H.7957 + S.2196 — Private Key Protection: Protect citizens' digital property rights
  • S.2021 — Tax Exemption: Exempt small Bitcoin transactions (up to $5,000/month) from state taxes

Why Act Now?

Rhode Island ranks #50 in WalletHub's "Best States to Start a Business" report. Bitcoin-friendly policies could differentiate our state, attract innovation, and create high-paying jobs. Texas, New Hampshire, and Wyoming are already reaping these benefits.
